What is the Pokédex?

The **Pokédex** is an electronic device that records data on Pokémon encountered by a trainer. It is a **vital tool** for every Pokémon trainer, providing detailed information on the various species in the Pokémon world.

In the Pokémon games, trainers use the **Pokédex** to collect data about Pokémon they’ve encountered during their journeys. As a trainer catches or sees more Pokémon, their Pokédex is updated with detailed entries about each Pokémon's habitat, abilities, and evolutions.

The **Pokédex** is also a helpful tool for trainers when deciding which Pokémon to capture next. It lists the habitats and locations of different Pokémon, which is essential for **catching rare or legendary Pokémon**. It is often considered a **Pokémon encyclopedia**, providing trainers with everything they need to know about the Pokémon they encounter.

For example, the **Kanto Pokédex** includes entries for the first 151 Pokémon, from **Bulbasaur** to **Mew**. In later regions, additional Pokémon were added, and the **Pokédex** grew in size and functionality. The Pokédex has evolved over time, and in modern games, it is often a feature within the **smartphone-like devices** of trainers.

Electric Pokémon

Pikachu: Pikachu is one of the most well-known Pokémon in existence...

Abilities: Static, Lightning Rod

Weakness: Ground-type moves

Other Electric Pokémon:

  • Jolteon: A sleek and agile Pokémon known for its sharp spikes and lightning-fast speed.
  • Zapdos: A legendary bird Pokémon, capable of controlling thunderstorms.

Fire Pokémon

Charizard: Charizard is a fierce and independent Pokémon, known for its ability to breathe fire...

Abilities: Blaze, Solar Power

Weaknesses: Water, Rock, Electric

Other Fire Pokémon:

  • Arcanine: Known as the "Legendary Pokémon," Arcanine is admired for its majestic appearance...
  • Blaziken: A Fire/Fighting-type Pokémon with high attack power and devastating moves like Blaze Kick.

Water Pokémon

Squirtle: Squirtle is a Water-type Pokémon that is often chosen as a starter Pokémon in the Kanto region. Squirtle is known for its ability to launch powerful water-based attacks like **Water Gun** and **Hydro Pump**. It evolves into Wartortle and then into Blastoise, which is capable of using **Hydro Cannon**, one of the most powerful Water-type moves.

Abilities: Torrent

Weakness: Electric-type moves

Other Water Pokémon:

  • Gyarados: Known for its transformation from the weak Magikarp into the mighty Gyarados. As a Water/Flying-type, it can unleash **Hydro Pump**, **Ice Beam**, and **Dragon Rage**. Gyarados is often feared for its devastating offensive abilities and intimidating appearance.
  • Milotic: A graceful Water-type Pokémon known for its beautiful scales. Milotic evolves from Feebas and has the ability to learn powerful healing moves like **Recover** and **Aqua Ring**, along with offensive moves like **Surf** and **Ice Beam**.
  • Vaporeon: A Water-type evolution of Eevee that can summon powerful Water-type moves like **Surf** and **Hydro Pump**. Vaporeon’s ability **Water Absorb** allows it to heal by absorbing Water-type moves used against it.
